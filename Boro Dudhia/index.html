Boro Dudhia

Local name: Boro dudhi, Boro kerui, Ghao-pata

English name: Garden spurge, milkweed, Ashtma Plant.

Scientific name: Euphorbia hirta

Family: Euphorbiaceae

Habit

Annual herb with a spreading or ascending growth habit.

Habitat

Found in pulse crops, sunhemp, fallow lands, roadsides, homesteads and bank of ponds.

Growing season

Rabi and Kharif.

Associated crops

Often grows as a weed in pulse crops, vegetables, and sugarcane fields.

Propagating organ

Seeds are the primary propagating organ.

Leaf

Opposite, elliptic-oblong to oblong-lanceoate, minutely toothed, nerves distinct.

Stem

Ascending, much branched from the base, branches often reddish, clothed with brownish crisp hairs, have milky sap.

Inflorescence

Cyathium, terminal or axillary, dense in cluster. Fruit: a capsule, three angled.

Root

Taproot system with fine lateral roots.

Control measures

Hand weeding or hoeing. Crop rotation and maintaining soil health. Application of pre- and post-emergence herbicides.
